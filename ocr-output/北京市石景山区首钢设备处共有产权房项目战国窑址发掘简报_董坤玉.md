Abstract: From September 3 to 19, 2020, the Beijing Municipal Institute of Archaeology conducted a rescue excavation
of kiln remains discovered during archaeological prospection for the Shougang Equipment Department Shared – Ownership Hous-
ing Project (involving requisitioned roads, partial green space, and residential land). The excavation revealed 4 kilns and 1
ash pit, yielding 130 artifacts primarily comprising pottery sherds. Based on the kiln structures, ash pit morphology, and arti-
facts, the site is preliminarily dated to the Warring States period. These findings provide crucial material evidence for studying
kiln technology and craft production in the Beijing area during the Warring States period.
